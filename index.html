This site introduces an open-source numerical pipeline for the construction developed by Alessandra Colla, Heinz-Peter Breuer, and Giulio Gasbarri in two recent papers in Physical Review A. It is offered as an open companion to that work — not a competing claim, not an endorsement, but a benchmarked numerical implementation made available under FAIR principles. We welcome correspondence from the original authors and from the wider open-quantum-systems community, both theory and experiment.

What the pipeline does

Starting from a microscopic open-system model, the pipeline computes the effective Hamiltonian K(t) of the time-convolutionless master equation under the Hayden–Sorce minimal-dissipation gauge, together with its perturbative expansion order by order, and the canonical generalised-Lindblad form of the corresponding generator. It is built around the basis-independent operational expression (Letter Eq. (6)) and the recursive perturbative construction introduced in the seed papers below.

The pipeline aims to expose the scope of applicability of the construction rather than to confirm it. Concretely, this means: side-by-side comparison against numerically exact and standard approximate methods, explicit identification of regimes where the perturbative hierarchy fails, structural-identity checks at every computed order, and tracking of the gauge dependence wherever competing effective-Hamiltonian descriptions are available.

At v0.2.0 + DG-2 PASS, the implementation that produces verdicts spans four packages — cbg (effective-Hamiltonian recipe, TCL recursion including the displaced-bath path, generalised cumulants D̄_1 / D̄_2 with profile-aware dispatch, and the Council-cleared displacement_profiles registry), models (system–environment factories), numerical (tensor primitives and time grids), and reporting (benchmark-card schema, loader, validator, dynamical and algebraic-map runners with thermal- and coherent-displaced dispatch). The auto-generated API documentation covers all four; the pedagogical tutorial remains available as the entry-level orientation document. What is presently verifiable is bounded by the validity envelope; the API documentation describes capabilities, not validation status.

Why this companion exists

The Colla–Breuer–Gasbarri construction turns a long-standing ambiguity in non-Markovian open-system dynamics — the non-uniqueness of the coherent/dissipative split — into a concrete, calculable object once the minimal-dissipation gauge is chosen. We think the construction deserves an open, benchmarked numerical companion that lets practitioners test it transparently, including in regimes where it might fail, and that makes the gauge dependence visible at every step.

We are explicit that K(t) is a coordinate-dependent object under the chosen gauge. Comparisons with alternative frameworks — Hamiltonian of mean force, polaron-transformed pictures, Mori-projector approaches — require explicit gauge alignment and are not adjudicated by this pipeline alone. Reports and plots from the pipeline carry the gauge annotation by default.

For the originating authors and the OQS community

If you are one of the originating authors, or if you work on related theory or experiment, this companion is an open invitation. We are interested in correspondence on at least three fronts: solvable-model test cases where the perturbative hierarchy can be cross-checked analytically; non-trapped-ion experimental platforms where the time-dependent K(t) signature could be tested independently of existing observations; and method comparisons against HEOM, TEMPO, MCTDH, pseudomode, and chain-mapping approaches that are likely to fail in regimes complementary to ours.

Disclosures

Conflict of interest. The maintainer of this repository (U. Warring) is a co-author of the trapped-ion experimental paper Colla, Hasse, Palani, Schaetz, Breuer, Warring, Nat. Commun. 16, 2502 (2025), which is cited by the seed papers as providing experimental fingerprints supporting the minimal-dissipation Ansatz. To contain this conflict, the pipeline treats data from that experiment as a secondary cross-check only, never as primary evidence; primary experimental benchmarks must come from independent groups. Where no independent data exist, the corresponding model carries an explicit "awaiting independent replication" annotation. We actively welcome independent fingerprinting on other platforms.

Scope of citation. This pipeline must not be cited as evidence that K(t) is the unique physical Hamiltonian of a strongly-coupled open quantum system. K(t) is coordinate-dependent under the Hayden–Sorce gauge; alternative gauges yield different effective Hamiltonians and different thermodynamic readings. Citation rules and the current scope of applicability are documented in the repository.

How to engage

Clone the repository, open an issue, or get in touch by email. Three forms of engagement are particularly valuable to us at this stage: independent replication of the trapped-ion observation on a different experimental platform; analytic cross-checks on solvable models where the perturbative hierarchy can be evaluated to high order; and method comparisons against techniques whose failure modes do not overlap with finite-environment exact diagonalisation or QuTiP master-equation solvers.
