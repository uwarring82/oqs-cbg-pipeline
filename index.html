Welcome

This repository is for readers who want to inspect, run, and challenge a concrete implementation of the minimal-dissipation effective Hamiltonian K(t). It is meant as a companion to the theory, not as a shortcut around it: every claim is bounded by frozen benchmark cards, a validity envelope, and an explicit gauge annotation.

We welcome correspondence from the originating authors and from the wider open-quantum-systems community. The most useful contributions at this stage are independent model checks, cross-method comparisons, and carefully scoped tests of where the perturbative hierarchy stops being reliable.

What the Pipeline Does

Given a TCL generator, the package computes the Hayden-Sorce minimal-dissipation effective Hamiltonian using the basis-independent expression from the Colla-Breuer-Gasbarri Letter. The implementation also carries the benchmark-card runner, model factories, cumulant helpers, thermal and coherent-displaced paths, and the post-verdict thermal-Gaussian n=4 route used for analytic follow-up work.

The project is deliberately conservative about claims. K(t) is coordinate-dependent under the chosen gauge, and the repository never treats it as a unique physical Hamiltonian of an open system. Comparisons to Hamiltonian-of-mean-force, polaron, Mori-projector, HEOM, TEMPO, MCTDH, pseudomode, or chain-mapping approaches need their own alignment and failure-mode analysis.

How To Engage

Clone the repository, open an issue, or use the GitHub templates for a bug report, DG-status-change proposal, or pull request. Contributions to scientific surfaces should keep the cards-first discipline intact: freeze parameters before writing verifying code, label failure modes, and state the validity-envelope impact.

Disclosures

Conflict of interest. The maintainer, U. Warring, is a co-author of the trapped-ion experimental paper cited by the seed papers as an experimental fingerprint of the minimal-dissipation Ansatz. The repository treats that experiment as a secondary cross-check only; primary experimental benchmarks should come from independent groups.

Citation scope. Do not cite this pipeline as evidence that K(t) is the unique physical Hamiltonian of a strongly coupled open system. Cite only the sub-claims authorised by the validity envelope, and include the Hayden-Sorce gauge and Ledger anchor where required.
