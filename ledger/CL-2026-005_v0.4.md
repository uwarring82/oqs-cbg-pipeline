- **Companion.** A. Colla, H.-P. Breuer, G. Gasbarri, *Recursive perturbation approach to time-convolutionless master equations: Explicit construction of generalized Lindblad generators for arbitrary open systems*, Phys. Rev. A **112**, 052222 (2025). DOI: 10.1103/9j8d-jxgd.

# Framing claim (descriptive, unclassified)

The pair offers (i) an operator-basis-independent, closed-form expression for the effective Hamiltonian K(t) of any time-convolutionless (TCL) generator obeying the minimal-dissipation gauge, and (ii) a recursive perturbative series for K(t) and for the canonical TCL generator in left/right-acting form, valid order by order without restriction to Gaussian baths or linear couplings beyond the standard initial-product-state assumption.

Within Harbour vocabulary: this is a *gauge fix* on the coherent/dissipative split of a TCL generator, plus a *constructive recipe* for that gauge. It does not propose new physics; it sharpens the bookkeeping in a regime (non-Markovian, strong-coupling) where the bookkeeping is otherwise ambiguous. Stated more carefully: the contribution is best understood as a projection choice on the generator space rather than a dynamical law; its physical meaning is therefore mediated by the choice of projection. This places the construction structurally adjacent to gauge fixing in field theory, the choice of thermodynamic potential, and decompositions of generators in non-Hermitian dynamics — i.e. it is a *map* in the Harbour sense, not a territory claim.

# Entry 1 — Operational expression for K(t)

**A. Claim** [fidelity: HIGH — paraphrase preserving authors' explicit formulae].
For any Hermiticity-preserving, trace-annihilating generator L on a d-dimensional system, the effective Hamiltonian fixed by the Hayden–Sorce minimal-dissipation gauge admits the basis-independent form K = (1/2id) Σ_α [F_α†, L[F_α]] for any Hilbert–Schmidt orthonormal operator basis {F_α}. In the matrix-unit basis this reduces to K = (1/2id) Σ_{j,k} [|j⟩⟨k|, L[|k⟩⟨j|]] (Letter Eqs. (6)–(7)).

**B. Predictions / consequences.**
1. Applied to a generator already in canonical Lindblad form with traceless jump operators, K returns the original Hamiltonian term (consistency check).
2. Applied to the Markovian weak-coupling generator, K reproduces the standard Lamb shift.
3. Applied to a pseudo-Kraus representation L[X] = Σ_i ω_ii V_i X V_i†, K reduces to the diagonal Hayden–Sorce 2022 expression — i.e. the new formula generalises the prior result to the off-diagonal (ω_{ij}) case.

**C. Constraints.**
- [THEORETICAL] Algebraic identity — derivation via the scalar product on htp(H) defined by Hayden–Sorce, evaluated using the fourth Haar moment (Letter Appendix B). No empirical input.
- [METHODOLOGICAL] Gauge dependence — the result is uniquely defined *only* once the minimal-dissipation gauge is imposed; the underlying ambiguity in the coherent/dissipative split is not removed in any deeper sense.

**D. Comparison.**
- *Prior:* Hayden–Sorce (2022) gave the projection but only an explicit formula in pseudo-Kraus form.
- *This work:* basis-independent form valid for arbitrary L ∈ htp(H).
- *No falsifying observation possible* — this is a theorem, not a hypothesis. The relevant question is whether the *gauge choice* itself is the right one, which is not adjudicated by the algebra.

**E. Classification.** **COMPATIBLE.**
The derivation is internally consistent, recovers known limits, and the consistency checks (canonical-form input, weak-coupling Lamb shift, pseudo-Kraus reduction) are exhibited. The classification is conditional on accepting the Hayden–Sorce gauge as the operative definition of "the" effective Hamiltonian — see Entry 7 for the gauge-choice question.

# Entry 2 — Recursive perturbative expansion for K(t) and for the TCL generator

**A. Claim** [fidelity: HIGH — paraphrase of explicit constructions in Letter Eqs. (14)–(18) and Companion Eqs. (19)–(28), (43)–(45)].
Given a microscopic Hamiltonian H = H_S + H_E + λ H_I with factorisable interaction H_I = A ⊗ B and uncorrelated initial state, the TCL generator admits a perturbative series L_t = Σ_n λⁿ L_n, expressible in a generalised Lindblad form with traceless jump operators at every order, and the corresponding effective Hamiltonian admits the matched series K(t) = Σ_n λⁿ K_n with explicit recursion. The "generalised cumulants" D̄(τ_1^k, s_1^{n−k}) obey a recursion (Letter Eq. (17); Companion Eq. (27)) that builds order n from orders < n.

*Scope clarification.* "Arbitrary open systems" in the Companion's title refers to arbitrary system / environment / interaction structures *within the TCL construction* — i.e. wherever the inversion L_t = ∂_t Λ_t ∘ Λ_t^{-1} is well defined. It does *not* mean arbitrary non-invertible reduced dynamics; the standard TCL invertibility window applies and is acknowledged by the authors.

**B. Predictions / consequences.**
1. Explicit closed-form expressions through fourth order (Companion Sec. IV).
2. The structure is preserved without Gaussian or linear-coupling assumptions on the bath.
3. Initial system–environment correlations modify the dissipative inhomogeneity but leave K(t) invariant (Letter Appendix C; Companion §II.A).

**C. Constraints.**
- [THEORETICAL] Domain of validity. The Companion explicitly notes (Sec. II.C, footnote 1) that the inversion L_t = ∂_t Λ_t ∘ Λ_t^{-1} requires ∥Λ_t − id∥ < 1, i.e. the standard TCL invertibility window. Singularities outside this window are flagged but not resolved in this work.
- [THEORETICAL] Convergence. The Companion is explicit that "convergence of the perturbative series depends on system parameters, and singularities in the exact TCL generator can arise in specific regimes" (citing Vacchini & Breuer, PRA 81, 042103 (2010)).
- [METHODOLOGICAL] Initial-state assumption. Product initial state ρ_S ⊗ ρ_E. Generalisation to correlated initial states is treated only at the level of the inhomogeneous term, with K(t) shown invariant.

**D. Comparison.**
- *Prior:* Gasbarri & Ferialdi, PRA 97, 022114 (2018) — recursive expansion in commutator/anticommutator form.
- *This work:* extension to left/right-acting form, which is what permits the canonical (generalised Lindblad) regrouping with traceless jump operators; this is the structural advance.

**E. Classification.** **COMPATIBLE, scope-limited.**
The construction is mathematically explicit and consistency-checked through fourth order. The label is *not* "COMPATIBLE, universal" because the convergence question is open and is acknowledged as such by the authors. A claim of universal applicability in the strong-coupling regime would be UNDERDETERMINED; the actual claim made — order-by-order existence in the canonical form — is compatible with the cited theoretical constraints.

# Entry 3 — Pure-dephasing structural result

**A. Claim** [fidelity: HIGH — direct algebraic result in Letter Eq. (19) and Appendix D].
For a spin-½ system with H_S = (ω/2) σ_z and interaction operator A = σ_z, the effective Hamiltonian retains the form K(t) = (ω_r(t)/2) σ_z; even-order contributions vanish, and odd-order contributions are proportional to σ_z (Letter Eq. (19); proof in Letter Appendix D).

**B. Predictions / consequences.**
1. No environment-induced eigenbasis rotation in pure dephasing.
2. For environments whose initial state is diagonal in the Fock basis (in particular thermal states of a linearly-coupled bosonic bath), all odd-order cumulants of B vanish, hence K(t) reduces to (ω/2) σ_z — *no* renormalisation. This recovers the standard dephasing spin–boson exact result (Łuczka 1990; Doll et al. 2008; Leggett et al. 1987 [Ref. 42 in Letter]).
3. Time-dependent shifts are predicted whenever odd-order bath cumulants are nonzero (e.g. non-thermal or coherently-displaced environments).

**C. Constraints.**
- [THEORETICAL] Algebraic. Follows from A(τ_1^k, s_1^{n−k}) = ⟨σ_z^{n−k}⟩_{1/d} σ_z^k, whose parity structure forces the stated vanishing pattern.
- [METHODOLOGICAL] Comparison points (named in the paper): Seegebrecht & Schilling (J. Stat. Phys. 191, 34 (2024)) — dispersive spin–oscillator, time-dependent shift; Neves & Brito (Sci. Rep. 15, 33175 (2025)) — pure qubit–qubit dephasing, time-dependent shift. Both are reproduced by Entry 3's structural result.

**D. Comparison.** Result is consistent with all prior analytical and numerical findings cited; no contradiction surfaced in this review.

**E. Classification.** **COMPATIBLE.**

# Entry 4 — Unbiased-spin (A = σ_x) thermal-bath result

**A. Claim** [fidelity: HIGH — direct algebraic result in Letter Eqs. (D.4)–(D.6)].
For a spin–boson model with H_S = (ω/2) σ_z and orthogonal coupling A = σ_x to a thermal bosonic bath (Letter Eq. (22)), odd-order contributions to K(t) vanish and even orders contribute only diagonal terms; consequently K(t) ∝ σ_z and the eigenbasis of H_S is *not* rotated by the interaction.

**B. Predictions / consequences.**
1. Energy-level shift without eigenbasis rotation in the canonical thermal regime — i.e. the effective Hamiltonian remains diagonal in the σ_z basis at all orders.
2. Eigenbasis rotation is predicted *only* when odd-order bath cumulants are nonzero (e.g. time-asymmetric bath states, coherent displacements, non-equilibrium preparations).

**C. Constraints.**
- [THEORETICAL] Algebraic. Parity decomposition of A(t_1^m) under m even/odd (Letter Eqs. (D.5)–(D.6)) plus vanishing of odd cumulants for thermal Gaussian baths.
- [METHODOLOGICAL] Numerical anchor. Gatto, Colla, Breuer, Thoss, Phys. Rev. A 110, 032210 (2024) [Ref. 43 in Letter] had reported numerical evidence for absence of eigenbasis rotation in this regime. The present Letter provides the analytic proof.

**D. Comparison.**
- *Prior numerical conjecture:* Gatto et al. 2024.
- *This work:* analytic proof of the conjecture under the stated assumptions.
- The match is the cleanest validation in the paper that does not invoke an experimental result.

**E. Classification.** **COMPATIBLE.**

# Entry 5 — Even/odd structural distinction and second-order FDT interpretation

**A. Claim** [fidelity: HIGH for n=2 component; MEDIUM for higher-order extrapolation, which the authors explicitly leave as suggestive].
Even-order terms K_{2m} couple bath and system contributions of *opposite* time-ordering-reversal symmetry classes; odd-order terms K_{2m+1} couple contributions of the *same* symmetry class (Letter Eqs. (23)–(24)). At second order, this reduces to the linear-response decomposition K_2 ∝ ∫ dτ [S(t,τ) [A(t), A(τ)] / 2i − χ(t,τ)/4 · ({A(t), A(τ)} − ⟨{A(t), A(τ)}⟩_{1/d})], with S the symmetric (fluctuation) and χ the antisymmetric (dissipation) parts of the bath two-point function (Letter Eq. (25)).

**B. Predictions / consequences.**
1. The first-order term is a driving generated by ⟨B(t)⟩ — non-vanishing only when the bath has initial coherences relative to the interaction.
2. The second-order term decomposes into a fluctuation contribution (driving the antisymmetric part) and a dissipation contribution (driving the symmetric part) — a clean fluctuation–dissipation structure for the *coherent* renormalisation, not just the dissipator.

**C. Constraints.**
- [THEORETICAL] Internal — parity decomposition of D̄ and A under time-ordering reversal (Letter pp. 4–5).
- [THEORETICAL] External (citation only) — Kubo (J. Phys. Soc. Jpn. 12, 570 (1957)) for the linear-response identification.

**D. Comparison.** The second-order FDT identification is novel here in that it applies to K, not the dissipator. Higher-order analogues are *suggested* but not constructed; the authors say it "is reasonable to expect" higher orders to reflect more intricate environmental correlations.

**E. Classification.** **COMPATIBLE for second order; UNDERDETERMINED at higher orders.**
*Discriminant condition.* An explicit identification of higher-order multi-point cumulant structure (e.g. third-order response function in nonlinear-response theory) within the K_n expansion, matching the parity-symmetry classes derived at n=2.
*Feasibility tier.* **FEASIBLE-NOW (non-unique mapping).** The calculation is tractable using the Companion's explicit fourth-order expressions (Eqs. (60)–(78)), but identification with nonlinear-response objects is not canonical beyond second order and requires explicit choice of correspondence. The algebra is available; the mapping to response theory is a modelling decision, not a deduction. A successful higher-order identification would itself be a substantive result — it is the *choice of correspondence* that has to be argued, not the existence of fourth-order data.

# Entry 7 — Interpretive claim: "key conceptual link to strong-coupling quantum thermodynamics"

**A. Claim** [fidelity: MEDIUM — paraphrased reconstruction of a rhetorical move recurring across both papers' Introductions].
Both papers assert that the minimal-dissipation effective Hamiltonian provides "a key conceptual link to quantum thermodynamics in the strong-coupling and non-Markovian domains", with implications for thermal-machine performance metrics and for work–heat partitioning (Letter, Introduction; Companion, Introduction).

**B. Predictions / consequences.**
1. Time-dependent K(t) can play the role of a generator of work exchange between system and bath in the strong-coupling regime (cited: Picatoste, Colla, Breuer, PRR 6, 013258 (2024); Colla & Breuer, QST 10, 015047 (2025); Huang & Zhang, PRR 4, 023141 (2022)).
2. Performance metrics (efficiency, extractable work) acquire computable corrections from the renormalised K(t).

**C. Constraints.**
- [THEORETICAL] Competing frameworks for strong-coupling thermodynamics exist and are not all mutually consistent: Hamiltonian of mean force (Seifert, Talkner & Hänggi, etc.); Mori-projector approaches; the polaron-transformed picture; the present minimal-dissipation K(t). These define *different* effective Hamiltonians in general.
- [METHODOLOGICAL] The "key conceptual link" claim is gauge-conditional: it is the link *under the Hayden–Sorce gauge*. Under different gauges, the same dynamics admits different K and different thermodynamic readings.
- [THEORETICAL] No generally accepted invariant notion of "work" exists independent of the chosen system–environment partition and gauge; therefore, any thermodynamic interpretation based on K(t) is representation-dependent. K(t) cannot be reified as "the physical Hamiltonian" of the open system without an external choice that the construction itself does not provide.

**D. Comparison.** The papers do not adjudicate between competing strong-coupling thermodynamic frameworks; they identify the minimal-dissipation K(t) as one principled choice and compute it. This is a defensible scope, but the rhetorical claim ("key conceptual link") overshoots the technical claim ("a principled choice that admits a tractable expansion").

**E. Classification.** **UNDERDETERMINED.**
*Discriminant condition.* A test that distinguishes the minimal-dissipation K(t)'s thermodynamic predictions from a competing framework's predictions in the same physical setting. Candidates: comparison with Hamiltonian of mean force in a regime where the two disagree; comparison with non-equilibrium Green's-function-based work statistics in a driven non-Markovian setting.
*Feasibility tier.* **FEASIBLE-NOW** for solvable models (Caldeira–Leggett, Fano–Anderson, Jaynes–Cummings) — the Fano–Anderson route is the natural first calculation, leveraging Picatoste, Colla, Breuer, PRR 6, 013258 (2024) as the existing K-side baseline. **FEASIBLE-WITHIN-10Y** for general spin–boson finite-T comparison; not blocked, only effort-bound.
