# 2. What the Ledger entry is, in two paragraphs

The two Letters share authorship and topic. The first (the Letter) gives a basis-independent operational expression for the effective Hamiltonian K(t) of any time-convolutionless (TCL) generator obeying the Hayden–Sorce minimal-dissipation gauge, plus a recursive perturbative series in the system–bath coupling λ. The second (the Companion) develops the underlying TCL generator in canonical generalised-Lindblad form with traceless jump operators at every order, and supplies explicit fourth-order expressions. Together they offer a gauge fix on the coherent/dissipative split of a TCL generator, plus a constructive recipe for that gauge.

CL-2026-005 measures seven claims drawn from this pair: three theorem-level results (operational form for K; pure-dephasing structure; no eigenbasis rotation for σ_x coupling at finite-T thermal bath); one constructive perturbative result with an open convergence question (the recursive expansion itself); one mixed-order result (the even/odd parity decomposition with second-order fluctuation–dissipation interpretation); one cited experimental validation carrying a steward conflict (the trapped-ion result); and one interpretive claim about strong-coupling thermodynamics. Five entries are classified COMPATIBLE (with appropriate scope qualifiers); one is COMPATIBLE at second order and UNDERDETERMINED at higher orders; one is UNDERDETERMINED on a gauge-conditionality argument. None is INCONSISTENT.

# 3. Load-bearing decisions for Council deliberation

These are the substantive calls the Council is being asked to ratify or amend. Each is the kind of decision a future reader could reasonably contest if poorly grounded.

## 3.1 The gauge-conditional framing

The dossier locates the Colla–Breuer–Gasbarri contribution as a *projection choice on the generator space*, not a dynamical law (framing claim, paragraph 2). Within COMPATIBLE, three entries (1, 3, 4) are theorem-level under the Hayden–Sorce gauge; two are constructive under the same gauge (2, 5). The interpretive Entry 7 is held UNDERDETERMINED precisely because competing gauges (Hamiltonian of mean force, polaron, Mori) yield different K(t)'s and different thermodynamic readings, and the construction itself does not adjudicate.

**Decision the Council is asked to ratify.** That gauge-conditionality is the right framing, that the framing-claim sentence locating the work as a projection choice is appropriate Harbour cartography, and that Entry 7's UNDERDETERMINED is the correct hold state until a discriminant test is run.

## 3.2 The steward conflict on Entry 6

Entry 6 cites Colla, Hasse, Palani, Schaetz, Breuer, Warring (Nat. Commun. **16**, 2502 (2025)) as the experimental validation invoked by both Letters. The local steward of CL-2026-005 is a co-author of that paper. Per the Breakwater self-reference exclusion, Entry 6's classification has been set to "COMPATIBLE AS SOURCE-FAITHFUL; EMPIRICALLY CONSERVATIVE", with a sticky flag that propagates to any downstream coastline using this entry as a constraint.

**Decision the Council is asked to ratify.** That this handling is sufficient — i.e. that the Hasse et al. precedent (explicit flag, conservative classification, no aggregation downstream) generalises to the present case, and that no further measure (e.g. transferring stewardship to a non-conflicted party) is required.

## 3.3 The two UNDERDETERMINED holds with feasibility tiers

- **Entry 5** is COMPATIBLE at n=2 and UNDERDETERMINED at higher orders, with feasibility tier **FEASIBLE-NOW (non-unique mapping)**. The discriminant condition is an explicit identification of the parity-symmetry classes in the K_n series with nonlinear-response objects beyond second order.
- **Entry 7** is UNDERDETERMINED, with a split tier: **FEASIBLE-NOW** for solvable models (Caldeira–Leggett, Fano–Anderson, Jaynes–Cummings) and **FEASIBLE-WITHIN-10Y** for general spin–boson finite-T comparison. The discriminant condition is a direct comparison between the minimal-dissipation K(t) and a competing framework's prediction (Hamiltonian of mean force is the natural first candidate) in a setting where the two disagree.

**Decision the Council is asked to ratify.** That the discriminant conditions are well-posed and the feasibility tiers correctly characterise the cost of running them.
