# 2. The operationalisability gap, in two paragraphs

CL-2026-005 v0.4 Entries 3.B.3 (Letter Appendix D / spin–boson pure-dephasing model) and 4.B.2 (Letter Appendix D / spin–boson σ_x model) make second-order observable predictions about a *non-thermal* bath state — specifically, a coherently-displaced multi-mode bosonic bath where odd-order bath cumulants are nonzero. The predictions are: a time-dependent renormalisation `ω_r(t) − ω` (Entry 3.B.3) and an eigenbasis rotation away from σ_z (Entry 4.B.2). Both originate in the non-vanishing first-cumulant `D̄_1(t)` of the bath two-point function under the displaced state.

A coherent state on a *multi-mode* bath, however, requires a basis choice that the Letter does not pin down at the level of detail the cards need. The displacement amplitude `α` is a complex-valued field over the bath spectrum — not a single number — and the prediction's numerical value depends on which spectral profile of `α(ω)` is adopted. Cards A3 v0.1.0 and A4 v0.1.0 (now superseded by their v0.1.1 thermal-only successors) carried `bath_state.coherent_displaced` with `displacement_amplitude: 1.0`, but did not specify whether this represented a single mode at the cutoff frequency, a single mode at the system Bohr frequency, a broadband envelope shaped by the spectral density `J(ω)`, a Gaussian envelope at some `ω_d` with bandwidth `Δω`, or any other choice. The gap was surfaced in DG-1 Phase C.7 (commit `18f36bb`), where `cbg.cumulants.D_bar_1` for the displaced case raised with an explicit "convention not specified" routing message rather than implementing a homemade choice. The Steward chose deferral (path (b) of three considered; see Card A3 v0.1.1 `failure_mode_log[0]`) on disciplinary grounds: a homemade convention would have made DG-1 verify "our convention produces structure" rather than "the paper's prediction is reproduced". The Council is now being asked to clear a successor convention so that future cards can verify the prediction proper.

# 3. The candidate conventions

The convention space the Council is asked to consider has at least four physically natural candidates, ordered roughly from simplest to most general. The list is **non-exhaustive** and **non-prejudicial**: enumeration is for orientation; the Council is not bound to choose one of these and may direct the steward toward a Letter-Appendix-D transcription, a successor paper, or a fresh derivation. The encoding of any chosen convention in the card schema is an additive surface analogous to B2's `pseudo_kraus_offdiag_omega` extension.

## 3.1 Single-mode coherent state at the bath cutoff frequency `ω_c`

The displacement is concentrated on a single bath mode at `ω = ω_c`: `α(ω) = α_0 δ(ω − ω_c)`. `D̄_1(t)` reduces to a single-frequency oscillation at `ω_c`. Simplest in the cumulant module; produces a clean per-fixture closed form. **Risk:** the experimentally relevant case is rarely a δ-function; this convention may verify a structurally simple prediction that the Letter's broader claim does not depend on.

## 3.2 Single-mode coherent state at the system Bohr frequency `ω`

Same δ-function form as 3.1 but at the system frequency: `α(ω) = α_0 δ(ω − ω_S)`. Maximises the resonant interaction with the system; tests the parity-class theorem most sharply. **Risk:** picks a special detuning point; structural identities may not generalise off-resonance.

## 3.3 Broadband displacement matched to the spectral density `J(ω)`

`α(ω) ∝ √(J(ω))` (or another monotonic function of `J(ω)`, to be specified). Physically natural for a "coherently-displaced thermal-perturbation analog" picture, where the displacement spectrum follows the bath's own coupling profile. `D̄_1(t)` is then an integral over the bath spectrum with a fixed envelope. **Risk:** the implementation invokes the spectral density at the cumulant layer, doubling the Card's dependency on `cbg.bath_correlations` and inflating the per-fixture algebra; verdicts are not at machine precision in general (numerical-quadrature bounded).

## 3.4 Specified-bandwidth Gaussian envelope at `ω_d` with width `Δω`

`α(ω) = α_0 exp(−(ω − ω_d)²/(2 Δω²))`, with `ω_d` and `Δω` as additional frozen parameters per fixture. Most general; reduces to (3.1) as `Δω → 0` with `ω_d = ω_c`, to (3.2) as `Δω → 0` with `ω_d = ω_S`, and approximates (3.3) for an appropriate envelope match. **Risk:** introduces two new frozen parameters; cards become finer-grained; the same fixture set covers more physics but requires more per-case derivations.

## 3.5 Prerequisite step: Letter Appendix D transcription gates this clearance

**Updated post-Act-1 (2026-05-04) per Architect deliberation flag (D-i / M / EC).** Letter Appendix D contains the prior-art notation for the displaced-bath construction underlying Entries 3.B.3 + 4.B.2 and is therefore the natural primary source for the convention. As of v0.2.0 of this briefing, Appendix D has *not* been entered into the repository's `transcriptions/` directory. Council-3 ADM-EC deliberation Act 1 (2026-05-04) ratified that this is a **prerequisite, not an alternative**: transcription must precede any specific-convention clearance, mirroring the Hayden–Sorce 2022 transcription that gated B1 v0.1.0 / B2 v0.1.0. The disciplinary precedent — *source transcribed → card frozen against transcription → additive cards thereafter* — is the standing pattern for DG-2 work; issuing a convention clearance against a non-transcribed primary reference would establish an exception future stewards could legitimately invoke, eroding the cards-first ordering.

Three transcription outcomes are possible, and the Act 1 → Act 2 procedure (see §4.3 below and the deliberation transcript) routes each:

  1. **Appendix D pins the convention.** That convention is adopted; §4.3 selection is moot. Act 2 freezes a card directly against the transcribed convention.
  2. **Appendix D underdetermines the convention** (one of §3.1–§3.4 is consistent with Appendix D but Appendix D does not single it out). The Council reconvenes for Act 2 to choose between handlings (a), (b), (c) of §4.3.
  3. **Appendix D is ambiguous AND none of §3.1–§3.4 fits.** See §3.6 below.

## 3.6 Contingency: Appendix D ambiguity with no §3.1–§3.4 fit

**Added at v0.2.0 per Architect D-(iii) flag.** A real possibility in a Letter-format paper with appendix-only details is that Appendix D's notation is itself ambiguous *and* none of §3.1–§3.4 captures the intended construction. In that case the Act 1 / Act 2 procedure does not produce a determinate selection; further input is required before any clearance:

  - **Letter-clarification consultation.** A direct request to the authors of the Letter (Colla, Breuer, Gasbarri) for clarification of Appendix D's intended convention. If the authors specify a convention, that becomes the adopted convention and Act 2 proceeds as outcome (1) above.
  - **Successor-paper consultation.** If a successor paper from the same group has since published an explicit displacement convention, the Council may direct adoption of that convention as the repository's primary, with Appendix D treated as superseded prior art.
  - **Open hold.** If neither Letter-clarification nor successor-paper consultation produces a determinate convention, Entries 3.B.3 + 4.B.2 remain *not authorised* indefinitely; the validity envelope holds at DG-2 PARTIAL — 3 of 4 sub-claims PASS without prejudice, and any future steward may re-open the question if new prior-art surfaces.

The "open hold" branch is the disciplinary fallback. It is structurally analogous to CL-2026-005 v0.4 Entry 7's UNDERDETERMINED hold under the main briefing: when discriminants are not crisp, the holding state is acceptable.

## 4.1 The convention gap is convention-aware

**Updated v0.2.0 per Guardian D-(ii) flag** (replacing the v0.1.0 phrasing "operational, not interpretive"). The four candidate conventions in §3 agree on **qualitative structural identity** — the Letter's Appendix D structural claim that K_n acquires non-thermal structure when odd cumulants are nonzero holds under any of (3.1)–(3.4) — but they **disagree on numerical content**. Cards under §3.1 (single-mode at ω_c) and under §3.4 (Gaussian envelope at ω_d ≠ ω_c) produce *different per-fixture closed forms* for `D̄_1`, `D̄_3`, etc., and therefore different K-values for a card's verdict to compare against. The clearance is therefore **convention-aware throughout**: it does not adjudicate physically inequivalent claims, but it does select one numerical operationalisation of a structurally invariant prediction.

**Decision the Council is asked to ratify** *(Act 1 verdict: CLEARED with amendment, 2026-05-04)*. That convention selection fixes numerical content without altering qualitative structural identity, and that single-convention clearance is sufficient *under the chosen convention* to lift Entries 3.B.3 / 4.B.2 from "not authorised" to "authorised under the chosen convention". # 6. Card-naming convention

**Added at v0.2.0 per Council-3 ADM-EC Act 1 Architect D-(ii) flag.** Cards authored under this clearance use the naming pattern

  `B<N>-conv-<X>_v<MAJOR>.<MINOR>.<PATCH>.yaml`

where `<N>` is the next free DG-2 card index (currently 4, since B1, B2, B3 are taken) and `<X>` is the §3 sub-section number identifying the adopted convention (e.g. `B4-conv-3.1` for §3.1's single-mode at ω_c, `B4-conv-3.4` for §3.4's Gaussian envelope). If the convention is transcribed from Letter Appendix D directly and is not one of §3.1–§3.4, the card name uses `B4-conv-D` with the transcription file as the structural anchor.

**Why bake the convention into the name.** A single name such as `B4` would be structurally fragile: if the Council later authorises a card under a second convention (purely additive coverage per §4.2), a renaming pass would be required to disambiguate. The convention-suffixed naming pattern makes additive coverage mechanical — `B4-conv-3.1` and `B4-conv-3.4` co-exist as siblings without any retroactive editing — and preserves the cards-first / Risk #6 mitigation that DG-1 and DG-2 PARTIAL have established.

**Compatibility with existing card-id field.** The card schema's `card_id` field (SCHEMA.md v0.1.2) accepts the suffixed name verbatim; no schema change is required. The `_BASIS_BUILDERS`-style registry pattern in `reporting/benchmark_card.py` is the operational analog for the convention dispatch — handler registration would key on (convention name, fixture name) rather than fixture name alone.

**Specialisation under handling (c)** *(added at v0.3.0 per Council Act 2 verdict)*. Because Act 2 adopted handling (c) (convention-agnostic registry encoding) rather than (a) or (b), the naming pattern collapses from "one card per convention" to "one card with per-fixture profile tags". The concrete first-instance name is therefore `B4-conv-registry_v0.1.0.yaml`, with each test_case carrying a `displacement_profile: <registry_key>` field naming one of the four cleared registry profiles (`delta-omega_c`, `delta-omega_S`, `sqrt-J`, `gaussian`). Verdict reporting per profile remains atomic; the card's overall verdict reports per-profile verdicts without aggregation, paralleling the per-test-case verdict surface of B1 / B2 / B3. Should a future Council clearance extend the registry beyond v0.1.0 (see registry-clearance-gate sub-section below), additional profile keys add new fixtures under the same card name; the card name does not bake the registry version into the suffix.

## 6.1 Registry-clearance-gate

**Added at v0.3.0 per Council Act 2 Architect amendment.** The displacement-profile registry is **not** open to ad-hoc Steward-discretion modifications. Any extension of the cleared profiles beyond the four v0.1.0 keys (`delta-omega_c`, `delta-omega_S`, `sqrt-J`, `gaussian`) — whether addition of new profiles or removal of existing ones — requires fresh Council clearance, recorded in the Sail's revision log. This gate parallels the way new Ledger entries require Council clearance: it preserves Council-tier discretion over the registry surface in perpetuity, rather than letting the registry surface drift under the Steward's hand.

The registry-clearance-gate is the structural mechanism by which handling (c) "removes Steward discretion": the Steward selects no single convention, *and* cannot enlarge the registry without Council action. In disciplinary parallel to the Validity-Envelope's `validation_kernel` axis under EC-9 (where the Council clears a *set* of kernels per the EC-7 floor and individual measurements rotate through the cleared set), the Council clears a *set* of admissible displacement profiles and individual fixtures rotate through that set; verification coverage scales with the registry; no single-profile verdict carries the full weight of the entry.

The Council is asked to verify that this triple-flagging plus failure-mode enumeration plus the (c)-specific transposition is structurally sufficient for *this* clearance and to direct any further measure if not.
