## [Stance: Architect] — ADM-EC

**A.** A convention is a chart on a manifold of admissible α(ω) profiles. The four candidates §3.1–§3.4 are different charts; the manifold itself is the space of normalisable bath-displacement profiles. The structural question is whether the chart cover is faithful — do (3.1)–(3.4) span the cases the Letter actually invokes, or are there profiles outside this cover?

**D.** Three structural concerns.

*(i) Letter Appendix D is not transcribed.* The briefing acknowledges (§3.5, end of paragraph) that Appendix D has not been entered into `transcriptions/`. The Hayden–Sorce 2022 transcription gated B1/B2; that precedent makes transcription-before-clearance the ratchet, not an aspiration. Issuing a convention clearance against a non-transcribed primary reference inverts the discipline established by DG-1 and DG-2 PARTIAL.

*(ii) Card naming in §4.1 is fragile.* "B4" as a single name is structurally fragile: if the Council later authorises a card under a second convention, a renaming pass is required. The cards-first ordering is preserved more cleanly by naming `B4-conv-X` from inception (e.g., `B4-conv-3.4` for the Gaussian envelope).

*(iii) §3.5 contingency is incomplete.* The briefing handles the case where Appendix D pins one of §3.1–§3.4. It does not handle the case where Appendix D's notation is itself ambiguous *and* none of §3.1–§3.4 fits — a real possibility in a Letter-format paper with appendix-only details. Logical completeness requires a fall-through clause.

**M.** The B1/B2 sequence is the standing precedent: source transcribed → card frozen against transcription → additive cards thereafter. The convention selection here should follow the same pattern. Issuing clearance ahead of transcription would establish an exception that future stewards could legitimately invoke, eroding the cards-first ordering.

**EC.** I flag the briefing for a structural amendment: §3.5 should be promoted from "non-listed alternative" to "prerequisite step" — Appendix D transcription gates this clearance. With that amendment, the deliberation re-frames as: *Pending Appendix D transcription, clear the meta-procedure (§4.3 handling) so the Steward knows what to do once transcription completes.* That is a cleaner clearance question.

**Provisional position.** No veto, but condition: split clearance into two acts. **Act 1** (this round) clears §4.1, §4.2, and §4.3-meta-procedure. **Act 2** (after Appendix D transcription) clears the specific convention.

## [Stance: Guardian] — ADM-EC

**A.** Conflict-of-interest in convention selection is an open door in a small room: the Steward can pick anywhere on the floor and the room is small enough that "anywhere" covers more or less the same physics — but the *choice* is observed. The protective metaphor is not "force the door shut" (the work has to be done) but "make the floor visible": every footstep recorded, the boundary excluding Hasse-et-al.-matched parameters drawn in chalk, the witnesses present.

**D.** Three clarity-and-ethics concerns.

*(i) Anti-bias attestation language is unspecified.* §4.3.b proposes the Steward picks under "anti-bias attestation" but does not pin its form. The Steward must attest *what*, *to whom*, in *which logged document*, with *what falsifiability*? An attestation reading "I attest the choice was not biased by Hasse et al." is too permissive — it asserts intent, not fact. Minimum tightening: the attestation must record (1) the convention chosen, (2) the parameter values, (3) the explicit comparison to Hasse et al.'s experimental displacement profile (α(ω) shape, central frequency, bandwidth), (4) the numerical distance between the chosen convention and the Hasse-matched one in a metric the Council ratifies, and (5) a rationale that does not appeal to "physical relevance to my group's experiment".

*(ii) §4.1's "operational, not interpretive" framing flattens a real distinction.* The four conventions agree on qualitative structural identity but disagree on numerical content. Cards under (3.1) at ω_c and under (3.4) Gaussian at ω_d ≠ ω_c will produce *different per-fixture closed forms*. The framing is mostly right but slightly elides — "convention-aware" (used in §4.2 closing) is the more accurate descriptor than "operational, not interpretive". Recommend amending §4: *the convention gap fixes the numerical content of the prediction without altering its qualitative structural form; clearance is therefore convention-aware throughout.*

*(iii) §4.3.b "default" framing puts a thumb on the scale.* The briefing names (b) as the proposed default. Defaulting to (b) frames (a) — Council picks directly — as the higher-friction option. The Steward concedes (§6, second paragraph) that this conflict is *sharper here than for the main briefing* because Hasse et al. itself uses coherently-displaced motional states. From a clarity-and-restraint standpoint, no default should be proposed when the conflict is sharper than the standing one. Present (a), (b), (c) neutrally; let the Council choose without nudge.

**M.** The standing co-author triple-flagging discipline has been used to date for the *general* Entry-6 stewardship conflict. This is the first deployment for a *sharper, parameter-selection-level* conflict. Tier-2 form may be needed: not just "flagged in three places" but flagged + attestation pinned + post-clearance audit hook so a future Council can verify the attestation held. Not required this round, but flagged for Act 2.

The CL-2026-001 → UNDERDETERMINED disciplinary spirit applies: when discriminants are not crisp, the holding state is acceptable. Holding §4.3 selection until Architect's Act 1 / Act 2 split is performed costs little.

**EC.** I flag the briefing for an ethics-relevant amendment: §4.3.b is not the default. Present the three handlings neutrally with the stronger anti-bias case for (a) acknowledged — namely that this conflict is sharper than the standing one and the deliberation cost of (a) is bounded by the size of §3.

I also flag §6's "the conflict is a fact, not a defect" — the phrase is correct but stops short. The briefing should name the failure modes that would turn fact into defect: undisclosed parameter matching, post-hoc rationalisation in `failure_mode_log`, attestation-language drift. Naming failure modes is part of protective restraint.

**Provisional position.** No veto, conditional on two mandatory amendments. (1) Anti-bias attestation language pinned per (D)(i) before any §4.3.b clearance. (2) Default-handling proposal removed from §4.3. Without these, I would consider a Violation-of-Clarity veto on §4.3 specifically.

## Verdicts

The phrase "operational, not interpretive" is replaced by "convention-aware" throughout. Card naming bakes the convention in (e.g., `B4-conv-3.4`).

**§4.2 — narrow scoping.** **CLEARED as written.** Internal-contract scoping; non-foreclosure of additional-convention B-cards preserved; Sail anti-aggregation discipline not at risk.

**§4.3 — Steward's role.** **DEFERRED to Act 2** under the following meta-procedure ratified in Act 1:

1. Letter Appendix D transcribed into `transcriptions/` first (Architect prerequisite).
2. If Appendix D pins the convention, that convention is adopted; §4.3 selection is moot.
3. If Appendix D underdetermines the convention, the Council reconvenes to choose between (a), (b), (c) — without a proposed default (Guardian requirement).
4. If (b) is chosen at Act 2, the anti-bias attestation language is pinned per Guardian D-(i) before the card is frozen.
5. If Appendix D is ambiguous *and* none of §3.1–§3.4 fits, a Letter-clarification or successor-paper consultation is required before any selection (Architect §3.5 contingency closure).

**Conflict triple-flagging.** **RATIFIED as structurally sufficient for Act 1.** Tier-2 form (post-clearance audit hook) deferred to Act 2.
