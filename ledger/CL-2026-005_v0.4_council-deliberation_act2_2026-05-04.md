## [Stance: Integrator] — opening frame

The procedural state is clean. Transcription is the gate Act 1 set; the gate has been passed; the classification is the one Act 1 anticipated as the "underdetermined" branch (deliberation transcript §4.3 step 3). The agenda Act 1 specified for this branch is exactly what the steward has put on the table: select between (a), (b), (c), no Steward-proposed default.

One procedural pre-question before stance deliberation. The steward's §7 verification log notes that an independent cross-read is recommended but not yet performed. Act 1 surfaced this as a Guardian-tier mitigation but did not make it mandatory. Two procedural options:

- **Option α.** Defer Act 2 substantive deliberation until the cross-read is performed. Cleanest under conflict; introduces a delay of likely 1–2 weeks to find and brief a non-Letter-author cross-reader (Haße or a QSIM postdoc).
- **Option β.** Proceed with Act 2 substantive deliberation now, with verdicts conditional on the cross-read finding no transcription drift. If the cross-read surfaces a discrepancy that affects the classification, Act 2 verdicts are vacated and re-deliberated.

The procedural cost differential is small. The classification is "underdetermined" because the source material is *silent* on the displacement profile — a class of finding that is robust against typographical or formula-level transcription drift. (Drift would have to *manufacture* the absence of `α(ω)`, `J(ω)`, etc., which is structurally unlikely.) The conflict-mitigation case for Option α is therefore weaker here than it would be for a "pinned" classification, where transcription accuracy directly determines the convention adopted.

Recommend **Option β** with the conditional vacate clause. Floor opens to Guardian first this time (the conflict tier is sharper at Act 2 than at Act 1, so clarity-and-ethics has earlier-bind on the procedure), then Architect, then Integrator synthesis.

## [Stance: Guardian] — ADM-EC

**A.** The room is now smaller and the floor more visible than at Act 1. Transcription is on the table. The boundary excluding Hasse-et-al.-matched parameter choices is no longer chalk on the floor — it can be drawn in ink, because the convention either gets selected by the Council directly (a), gets selected by the Steward under a fixed attestation template (b), or gets sidestepped by encoding it as a registry surface (c). The protective question is which inking discipline best preserves the Steward's reputational integrity *and* the repository's verification integrity.

**D.** Five clarity-and-ethics observations.

*(i) On Integrator's procedural pre-question.* I accept Option β with the conditional vacate clause. The Guardian-tier mitigation surfaced at Act 1 was about transcription-drift-affecting-pinned-classification; "underdetermined" by absence is a structurally different finding and is robust to the failure mode the cross-read protects against. I would still ask the Steward to nominate a cross-reader within the same procedural cycle as Act 2 implementation — not as a gate, but as a closure of the Act 1 recommendation.

*(ii) Handling (c) "convention-agnostic encoding" is structurally more attractive than at Act 1.* At Act 1, (c) was characterised as "structurally premature" because the §3 candidate set had not stabilised and the source material had not been transcribed. Both conditions have changed. The transcription confirms §3.1–§3.4 are silent-compatible (no source-pinning eliminates any of them), and the §3 set has been stable since v0.1.0 of the briefing. (c) now has the disciplinary advantage that *the steward never selects a convention at all*: the card encodes the convention as a parameterised surface, the Council clears the registry of admissible profiles, and individual fixtures are run under each registered profile. The conflict-of-interest dimension of §4.3 essentially evaporates — the Steward never picks one, so cannot pick the Hasse-matched one.

*(iii) Handling (a) "Council picks directly" remains the strongest single-convention discipline.* If the Council prefers a single-convention card over a registry card (for cards-first ordering reasons, or to keep the per-fixture algebra simple), (a) is the cleanest. Deliberation cost is bounded by §3's four candidates; the Council can pick by structural-simplicity criterion (favouring §3.1) or by parity-class-theorem-test-sharpness criterion (favouring §3.2) or by physical-naturalness criterion (favouring §3.3) without any Steward input.

*(iv) Handling (b) "Steward picks under attestation" is now the *weakest* option.* The transcription confirms the source material gives the Steward *no neutral basis* for picking — none of §3.1–§3.4 is supported or excluded by Appendix D. Any Steward selection is therefore a discretionary act with no anchor in the primary source. Under that constraint, the attestation template's item (5) — "rationale that does not appeal to physical relevance to my group's experiment" — has very little remaining ground to stand on. What other ground would the Steward appeal to? "Structural simplicity" is a Council-tier judgment; "literature canonicity" is a non-decision in the absence of canonical pinning; "personal mathematical preference" is not falsifiable. Handling (b) at Act 1 was framed as "Steward picks under explicit anti-bias constraint"; at Act 2 the constraint has effectively eaten the basis for picking.

*(v) On the Steward's reputational integrity.* This deserves explicit framing. The Steward is conflicted not because of malfeasance but because of domain depth — the same expertise that makes him the right person to steward this Ledger entry also makes his selections in this convention space inevitably entangled with his group's experimental work. Handling (a) and (c) both *protect* the Steward from a future audit that asks "why did you pick X?" by ensuring he didn't pick. Handling (b) *exposes* the Steward to that audit. The disciplined move is to choose the option that the Steward's future self will be most comfortable defending; that is (a) or (c), not (b).

**M.** The B1/B2 precedent at Act 1 was about source-transcription-before-card-freezing. The relevant precedent at Act 2 is the standing conflict-mitigation discipline across the Open-Science Harbour: when discretion can be removed, remove it. This appears in the Sail's anti-aggregation (the Ledger does not aggregate to avoid steward-discretion in weighting), in the validity-envelope discipline (no silent generalisation = no steward-discretion in scope), in the EC-9 kernel-orientation audit (kernel rotation = removing discretion in single-axis interpretation). Each of these instances chose the structural mechanism that minimised steward discretion. The same disciplinary logic favours (a) or (c) here.

**EC.** I flag Option β's conditional vacate clause as appropriate; I flag handling (b) as ethics-disfavoured at Act 2 by the argument in D-(iv); I flag the cross-reader nomination as a procedural close-out item.

**Provisional position.** No veto. Substantive recommendation: **prefer (c), with (a) as fallback if the Council judges registry-encoding too schema-heavy.** Strongly disfavour (b).

## [Stance: Architect] — ADM-EC

**A.** The structural manifold has now been mapped at two layers: the convention manifold (§3 candidates) and the implementation manifold (handlings a/b/c). The transcription pinned neither; both remain open. The architectural question is whether to collapse one or both manifolds at this clearance, and what schema and code surface each collapse implies.

**D.** Four structural observations.

*(i) Schema cost of (c) is bounded and one-time.* Handling (c) requires a registry surface in the card schema. The pattern is well-established: the existing `_BASIS_BUILDERS` registry in `reporting/benchmark_card.py` is the operational analog. Adding a `_DISPLACEMENT_PROFILES` registry indexed by profile name (e.g. `delta-omega_c`, `delta-omega_S`, `sqrt-J`, `gaussian`) keyed to a function that returns `α(ω)` given fixture parameters is a single PR-sized addition. Per-fixture, the card declares `displacement_profile: <name>` and the registry dispatches. This is *less* schema surface than each of the §3 candidates would have required as separate cards under (a) or (b), because the registry collapses the four would-be-separate code paths into one.

*(ii) Cards-first ordering under (c).* The card-naming convention §6 generalises cleanly: `B4-conv-3.1_v0.1.0`, `B4-conv-3.2_v0.1.0`, etc. become *fixture variants* under a single `B4-conv-registry_v0.1.0` card, each fixture tagged with its `displacement_profile`. Verdict reporting per profile remains atomic. The cards-first ordering is preserved; it is in fact *strengthened*, because the registry forecloses the renaming pass that single-name `B4` would have required when a second convention surfaces.

*(iii) Risk under (c): schema-surface drift.* The registry must be Council-cleared *as a registry*, not as four separate conventions. If the registry is opened to ad-hoc additions by future stewards without Council clearance for each addition, the discipline degrades. This is a real risk; mitigation is straightforward: the registry has a Council-clearance gate at the schema level (registry additions require Council clearance, recorded in the Sail's revision log), parallel to how new Ledger entries require Council clearance.

*(iv) On Guardian's D-(iv) handling-(b) collapse argument.* I find the argument structurally sound. Under "underdetermined", handling (b)'s anti-bias attestation has nothing to attest *to* — the attestation items 1–4 (convention, parameters, comparison to Hasse et al., numerical distance) are evaluable, but item 5 (rationale that does not appeal to physical relevance to my group's experiment) requires a positive ground for the rationale, and the source material provides none. (b) was designed for a case where the Steward would have a primary-source-anchored ground for the choice (e.g. "Appendix D suggests but does not pin §3.4; I picked §3.4 for the §3.4-suggesting reasons"). That ground has not materialised. (b) at Act 2 is therefore not just ethics-disfavoured but *structurally underdetermined* in the same sense as the convention itself.

**M.** The standing precedent for "registry of admissible options under Council clearance" is the Validity-Envelope's `validation_kernel` axis under EC-9: the Council does not pick a single kernel orientation per measurement, it clears a *set* of kernels (≥3 non-commuting per the EC-7 floor) and individual measurements rotate through the cleared set. Handling (c) is structurally the same move: the Council clears a *set* of admissible displacement profiles, and individual cards run all of them. Verification coverage scales with the registry; no single-profile verdict carries the full weight of the entry.

**EC.** I flag handling (b) as structurally underdetermined per D-(iv); I flag handling (c) as schema-tractable per D-(i)–(ii) with the registry-surface-drift mitigation per D-(iii).

**Provisional position.** No veto. Substantive recommendation: **prefer (c), with (a) as fallback. Concur with Guardian on disfavouring (b).** Add a structural amendment if (c) is selected: registry additions require Council clearance, recorded in the Sail's revision log.
