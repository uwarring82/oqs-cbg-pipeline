The `error = 0.0` outcomes reflect the **structural exactness** of the verified predictions, not a numerical noise floor:

- Letter Eq. (6) recovery of the input `H` term from a canonical Lindblad with traceless jump operators is exact at machine precision (Card A1.B.1; the dissipator contributes zero to K).
- The combined Hamiltonian recovery for Markovian weak-coupling with explicit Lamb shift is also exact (Card A1.B.2).
- The spin–boson trivialisation result has zero K_2 contribution by the parity cancellation in the [σ_z, [σ_z, X]] structure (Card A3.B.2; Łuczka 1990 / Doll 2008 / Leggett 1987 exact result).
- The Letter Eqs. (D.4)–(D.6) parity-class theorem gives zero transverse (σ_x, σ_y) component in K_2 for thermal bath even with orthogonal coupling A = σ_x (Card A4.B.1; the structural prediction Gatto et al. 2024 had reported numerically).

The cards' acceptance thresholds (1e-10 for A1; 1e-8 for A3, A4) are scaffolded to allow numerical-integration error margin; the actual results sit ~10+ orders of magnitude below them because the structural cancellations are algebraic, not numerical.
