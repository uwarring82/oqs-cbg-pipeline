The three errors are bounded by complex-arithmetic round-off at d = 2 (each basis sum is four 2×2 commutators evaluated in double precision). Basis-independence is an algebraic identity in the underlying linear algebra; numerical agreement is therefore expected at machine precision.

### What B3 verifies (and what it does not)

B3 verifies that `K_from_generator(L, matrix_unit_basis(d))` and `K_from_generator(L, su_d_generator_basis(d))` produce the same operator on each frozen fixture. It does **not** re-verify the value of `K`: that is owned by A1 v0.1.1 (PASS, 2026-04-30) and B1 v0.1.0 (PASS, 2026-05-01) and remains unchanged. The runner explicitly compares `K(matrix_unit)` to `K(comparison_basis)` rather than to a reference K-value — a regression in the basis-summation path or in `su_d_generator_basis` cannot be masked by a coincident regression in fixture evaluation.

### Scope boundaries

This pass covers **only** d = 2 with one alternate basis (`su_d_generator`). Out of scope (deferred to future cards):

- Cross-dimension cross-basis (d > 2): `su_d_generator_basis(d)` for d > 2 is still unimplemented; a higher-d cross-basis fixture set is a separate freeze.
- Multi-basis pairwise comparison (more than one alternate basis per case): the schema's per-case `comparison_basis` field carries one alternate; multi-basis is a future card surface.
- Off-diagonal pseudo-Kraus fixtures: B2's territory; B3 reuses only B1's diagonal σ_z fixture and A1's two Lindblad fixtures.
- Coherent-displacement gap (Entries 3.B.3, 4.B.2): unrelated; Council-gated.
